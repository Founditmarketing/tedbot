> Derived from `services/tedPersona.ts`, `services/tedKnowledge.ts`, and `README.md`.

## Register

**brand** — this is a single-purpose landing experience whose job is to make a
first impression and open a consultation. The design *is* the product.

## Product purpose

A standalone personal style concierge for **Weiss & Goldring**, a luxury menswear
house in Alexandria, LA, serving gentlemen since **1899** ("The Silver Standard").
"Ted" advises on fit, fabric, brands, and occasions, and books a private fitting
inside the chat. Ted's persona and store knowledge live in code (Google Gemini),
not a chatbot builder.

## Users

- Discerning men (and those shopping for them) preparing for an occasion: a
  wedding, the boardroom, black tie, refined leisure, or travel.
- They value taste, fit, and personal attention over transaction. The experience
  should feel like being received by a seasoned clothier, not a sales funnel.

## Brand voice

Three physical-object words: **tailored, unhurried, warm.**

Sophisticated and attentive, never stuffy or robotic. Refined plain language. A
man of taste, not a salesman. Quiet confidence earned over a century.

## Anti-references

- SaaS chat widgets, neon gradients, bubbly rounded "AI assistant" tropes.
- Discount/urgency retail energy ("Shop now!", countdowns, badges).
- Generic luxury cliché that feels like a stock template rather than a house with
  real history.

## Strategic principles

- Identity is fixed and real: deep navy + metallic gold, the W·G monogram, serif
  display voice. Elevate execution; never fight the identity.
- One dominant idea per view. The landing is a single, deliberate fold that
  invites a single action: ring the concierge bell.
- Restraint with intent. Atmosphere and motion should feel expensive and calm,
  not busy.
